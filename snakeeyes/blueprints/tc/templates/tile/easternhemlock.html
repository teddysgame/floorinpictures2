Interior Advice

Expert Review: Mr. Tee - Interior Consultant
4/5

Eastern Hemlock is the darkest colour among the Elder Brilliance series. This is the last threshold before going into a darker series. It is suitable for use in living rooms for those who like a touch of nature and wood feel. Unless specifically themed, it may not be a preferable choice for commercial areas as the colour looks mature and elderly for some people.


Consider to use this colour tile if:

  • You want your floor to seem larger. Light coloured floors tend to make the floor space look much larger.
  • You have large active pets such as dogs or cats at home. Light coloured floors hide scratches better from claw marks....
  • You have light coloured cabinets or furnitures. The blend of colours of wood, beige and white goes well together.
  • Your toilet accessories such as wash basin or toilet bowl is white.
  • You have less time maintaining your floor. Darker coloured tiles requires more attention and more frequent cleaning.
  • Also consider whether are you living in here for the long term. Lighter coloured tiles is a better preference to buyers.
  • If your wall colour is already dark coloured. Darker colours already make the space look smaller, so you may want to contrast it with a light coloured floor to prevent the space from shrinking even further.

Backstory & Origins

The eastern Hemlock also known as Tsuga Canadensis is native to eastern north America and is the state tree of Pennsylvania, America. It is also known as pruche du Canada in the French speaking regions of Canada. Its lumbers are widely used in general construction making box crates and pulp in paper manufacturing.
