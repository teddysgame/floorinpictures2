Interior Advice

Expert Review: Mr. Tee - Interior Consultant
4/5

Tupelo wood is a thick 5mm light grey coloured tile. It is much softer to step on. This tile goes well with white coloured walls and white coloured furnitures in our houses. It is also suitable to be used in bathrooms where most toilet accessories are white porcelain. It should go well with light coloured kitchen cabinets but try to refrain from going with dark coloured furnitures.


Consider to use this colour tile if:

  • You want your floor to seem larger. Light coloured floors tend to make the floor space look much larger.
  • You are worried for gappings to occur. Check out our FAQ for gapping issues.
  • You have large active pets such as dogs or cats at home. Light coloured floors hide scratches better from claw marks....
  • You have light coloured cabinets or furnitures. The blend of colours of grey and white goes well together.
  • Your toilet accessories such as wash basin or toilet bowl is white.
  • You have less time maintaining your floor. Darker coloured tiles requires more attention and more frequent cleaning.
  • Also consider whether are you living in here for the long term. Lighter coloured tiles is a better preference to buyers.
  • If your wall colour is already dark coloured. Darker colours already make the space look smaller, so you may want to contrast it with a light coloured floor to prevent the space from shrinking even further.

Backstory & Origins

Tulepo with its genus name Nyssa is a deciduous tree with simple leaves.
Nyssa is a Greek water female spirit or nymph also known as Naiads. They preside over water places and inhibited in swamps, streams, wells, mashes and ponds. Tupelo however is of Native American origin that derived from Creek of ito (tree) and opilwa (swamp) and was generally used during the 18th century.
Tulepo is also the 7th largest city north of Mississippi with an estimated population of 39 thousand residence. Fun fact: Elvis Presley was born here.
