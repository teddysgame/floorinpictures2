Backstory & Origins

Hazel trees are extensively grown due to their popular, edible nuts as an all-time favourite snack – Hazelnut. It is used extensively as snacks and in chocolates. The Hazel is a genus to the deciduous trees native to the temperate Northen Hemisphere.
The Celts, an Indo-European ethnolinguistic group of Europe identified by their use of Celtic languages and cultural similarities believed that the hazelnut gives wisdom and inspiration to an individual.
Legend tells the tale of nine hazel trees that grew around a sacred pond. And when the Hazel tree bared their nuts, hazelnuts fell from their trees into the pond and were eaten by salmons.


The salmon fish were also sacred to druids however one day a druid teacher in his quest for omniscient caught one of these salmon and asked his student to cook it but not to eat it.
While the boy was cooking the fish, he developed a blister on his thumb which he proceeds to suck it. It was then wisdom was transferred from the salmon fish to the boy who eventually went on to become one of the most heroic leaders in Gaelic mythology. This boy’s name was Fionn Mac Cumhail also famously known as Fin McCool.
According to "The Hazel Branch" from Grimms' Fairy Tales, hazel branches offered the greatest protection from snakes and other vermin that creeps the earth.
