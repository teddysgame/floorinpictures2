Backstory & Origins

The Boxelder Wood, Acacia melanoxylon also commonly known as the Australian boxelderwood, belongs to the Acacia species native to south eastern Australia. Some other names for Boxelder Wood is also known such as the Tasmanian boxelderwood, hickory or boxelderwood acacia.

Acacia melanoxylon is an ornamental tree valued and used in the making of cabinets, musical instruments, furnitures and even boats due to its decorative pattern. Due to it, it is now intentionally grew in forestry in countries such as Asia, Africa, Europe, South America and the United States.

Musical instruments made from Acacia melanoxylon in particular is guitars, drums, ukuleles, violin bows and organ pipes are highly valuable.
