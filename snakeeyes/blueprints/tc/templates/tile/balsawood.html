Interior Advice

Expert Review: Mr. Tee - Interior Consultant
4/5

Balsa Wood is a light grey coloured tile. This tile goes well with white coloured walls and white coloured furnitures in our houses. It is also suitable to be used in bathrooms where most toilet accessories are white porcelain. It should go well with light coloured kitchen cabinets but try to refrain from going with dark coloured furnitures.


Consider to use this colour tile if:

  • You want your floor to seem larger. Light coloured floors tend to make the floor space look much larger.
  • You have large active pets such as dogs or cats at home. Light coloured floors hide scratches better from claw marks....
  • You have light coloured cabinets or furnitures. The blend of colours of grey and white goes well together.
  • Your toilet accessories such as wash basin or toilet bowl is white.
  • You have less time maintaining your floor. Darker coloured tiles requires more attention and more frequent cleaning.
  • Also consider whether are you living in here for the long term. Lighter coloured tiles is a better preference to buyers.
  • If your wall colour is already dark coloured. Darker colours already make the space look smaller, so you may want to contrast it with a light coloured floor to prevent the space from shrinking even further.

Backstory & Origins

Balsa wood also known as the Ochroma tree is fast growing tree that reaches up to 30m tall. They are a genus that belongs to a mallow family Malvaceae.

It is a light weight material that is native to southern Brazil, northern Bolivia, and also southern Mexico. It can also be found in countries such as Indonesia, Thailand, Papua New Guinea, and the Solomon Islands.

Balsa wood will produce flowers during its 3rd year during rainy seasons.
