Interior Advice

Expert Review: Mr. Tee - Interior Consultant
4/5

Unlike the other vinyl tiles that uses glue to lock the tiles into place, Burma Paduak uses an interlocking mechanism to install and hold one another in place. It is commonly compared directly to laminated floorings. This tile also comes with an underlay so you do not need to lay additional underlayments. Use this tile if you want to DIY and have an easier installation of vinyl tiles. The reason most people choose this over laminated floorings is, although 7mm thick, this tile can still be cut by blade and does not need a to cut by a jigsaw cutter machine. Because it has a thickness of 12mm, it is relatively warm to step on and will be comfortable for toddlers or elderlys. Use this tile as well if you have a heavier traffic in the area to mask stains and dirt resulting from the traffic. However scratches will be more apparent so do take precautionary steps in securing sharp objects such as furnitures to not puncture the flooring surface. This tile is suitable for living rooms, kitchens and also bathrooms.

Consider to use this colour tile if:

  • You want to DIY without gluing.
    • You do not own a jigsaw cutter machine or intent to invest in one for your flooring purpose.
  • You want to skip laying the underlayment process.
  • You have bright coloured walls and want to constrast the colour of your floor.
