It highly depends on your type of subfloor. Different subfloor have different methods to test.
Concrete subfloor

  • Test the concrete subfloor by taping the floor and leaving it for 72 hours. If the tape is easily peeled off, the floor is too damn for vinyl and has to be insulated.
  • Clean the floor of debris and dust preferably using a vacuum.
  • Use a carpenter’s level or anything long (1 meter) and straight to locate uneven surface levelness.
  • Use floor leveling compound if necessary.
  • Sand off the floor if leveling compound is used.

Wooden subfloor
  • Clean the floor of debris and dust preferably using a vacuum.
  • It is encourage to lay plywood panels as underlayment.
  • Ensure there are some spacing at the corner for expansion and shrinkage.
  • Any bumps or irregularities in its surface can cause the vinyl flooring to wear, or prone to tear.

Step 1

  • Get the right measurements. Unlike tiles which may allow for wastage up to 10%, a skirting’s wastage would be quite substantial. The proper rule of thumb is to follow the saying ‘measure twice, cut once.’

Step 2
  • For angles, always cut your skirting at 45 degrees. Use a miter box to help with getting the perfect angle.

Step 3
  • Cut the coping or scribbing joints. A scribbing joint is where 2 skirting boards meet at the inner corner. This part is slightly more difficult but it can be done. Do check out our Youtube video below.

Step 4
  • Cut and attach the skirting boards with nails. Refrain from using a hammer if you can. You may use a nail gun for quicker and safer installation.

Step 5
  • Use putty to cover up nail holes.

Generally you should always install your vinyl floorings first before you install your cabinets. Here is why.

  • You are able to change your cabinet footprint later without the need to retile.
  • It looks a lot cleaner without the end profiles.
  • You may accidentally scratch your cabinet during installation.
  • Some people would save some money on the floorings that is covered by the cabinets however it is not recommended.
  • Unlike timber floorings that lifespan may not be as long as the cabinet and may need retiling later, vinyl tiles generally have longer lifespan and will not need any replacement before cabinets.
  • The workmanship will be a lot better since there will not be working in a limited working space and its less complicated especially on the scribbing part.
  • You do not need to worry on blocking off water spill.

This is a very subjective issue. You should only consider installing your cabinets first only if you are using floating vinyl floorings. This is because vinyl floorings contract and expand and may cause the flooring to buckle. However if you have a stable weather (without 4 seasons), it is not known to have contraction and expansion that big to cause buckles around cabinets. However do advise your installer on this matter.
