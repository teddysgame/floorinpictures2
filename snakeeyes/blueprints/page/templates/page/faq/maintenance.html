Gappings are long trenches arising from vinyl flooring tiles especially from the end to end side.

Gapping is a common problem that happens in vinyl floorings. The tiles tend to slide during contracting and expanding leaving a trench as shown. It is also a sign that the tiles were not tightly locked during the installation process. Glued down vinyl floorings may also have this problem if tiles are not glued properly or ‘slide’ during installation while weakening the glue.

Do

  • Wipe spills immediately and refrain from exposing to too much water.
  • Vacuum or sweep as often as possible. Sandy floors may scratch and wear the tiles over time.
  • Use floor protector for your furniture to prevent puncture or scratch damage.
  • Mop your floor with a suitable floor cleaner. You may check out our recommended cleaners on our site here.

Don't
  • Never use a detergent that is abrasive.
  • Do not use vinegar or shin products as it may leave a film instead.
  • Never use wax polish
  • Never use scrubbing tools like stainless steel brush.
  • Don’t use rolling casters at home such as computer chairs. Use a mat beneath it.
  • Never use steam mops for cleaning as it will cause bubbling.
